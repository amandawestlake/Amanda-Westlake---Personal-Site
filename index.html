Hey there! My name is Amanda (she/her), and I study Computer Science and Political Science at Tufts University. I've worked as a field organizer and intern on various political campaigns at the local, state, and national levels, and am currently the managing editor of the Tufts Observer, a biweekly magazine and Tufts's oldest publication. I'm interested in the intersections of politics and technology, tech for good, journalism, and climate justice advocacy; above all, I believe in the power of stories, data, and design to shift hearts and minds and enact progressive change.

Some of my proudest accomplishments are my work as a field organizer on the Ed Markey campaign and as a co-founder and leader of Students for Markey, and my writing and editing with the Observer. Watch my video with Senator Markey and learn about the phone bank goal thermometer I created that's been used and shared by campaigns across the country! At Tufts, I'm also a hub coordinator of the Sunrise Movement and a teaching assistant for Data Structures (CS15). This summer, I'm thrilled to be empowering young people to close the gender gap in tech as a TA with Girls who Code's Summer Immersion Program. In my free time, I enjoy reading, crossword puzzles, ceramics, Tetris, and antiquing.

Email me at Amanda.Westlake@Tufts.edu!
